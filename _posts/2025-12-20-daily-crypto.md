### Market Overview

**Spot Prices & Market Cap** — Bitcoin traded between \$86,846–\$89,400 over the past 24h, settling near **\$88,334 (+0.38% 24h)**, with intraday recovery visible. Ethereum traded near **\$2,974–\$2,986 (+0.86–2.23% 24h)**, rebounding on sentiment improvement. Total crypto market cap climbed to **\$3.11 trillion (+4.34% 24h; +\$129.71B gain)**. Bitcoin dominance held **56.28%**; Ethereum **11.73%**.[^1][^2][^3][^4]

**Top Altcoins (24h)** — Gainers: **Aptos (APT) +11.78%**, **Zcash (ZEC) +9.83%**, **Uniswap (UNI) +9.83%**, **XRP +4.71%**, **Solana (SOL) +0.67%**, **Arbitrum (ARB) +4.40%**, **Optimism (OP) +6.13%**. Losers: **TRX ±0%**, **Mantle (MNT) -2.35%**. Notable: **APT, UNI, ZEC outperformance** suggests shift toward L2 and DeFi tokens.[^5]

**Derivatives** — BTC/ETH open interest declined **16% in November vs. October**, but positioning remained resilient. Funding rates for BTC and ETH were **positive 92% of Q3 2025**, peaking at **0.56% annualized** during selloffs. Recent aggregate BTC funding rose to **0.085%** (highest since Nov. 21), signaling long re-entry. **XRP funding surged 360% in 24h (Dec. 10) as open interest rose 3.18%**.[^6][^7][^8]

**Liquidations** — Early December saw **\$960M in liquidations as BTC fell \$92K→\$80.6K**, affecting **396K traders**. **72–83% long positions** liquidated across Bybit (\$109.3M), Binance (76%), Hyperliquid (83%). Nov 21 saw **\$2B liquidations in 24h** (391K traders affected), indicating high leverage exposure. Dec 20 showed no major spike; market stable.[^9][^10]

**Stablecoin Flows** — USDT maintained **\$30–50B weekly transfer volume** in Q3; USDC **\$10–25B**, signaling **1:2–3:1 dominance ratio**. September 2025 saw **coordinated burn activity** (\$9B+ weekly for each), indicating active supply management. **Tether froze \$3.3B USDT vs. Circle's \$109M** (2023–2025), revealing **30x policy gap**.[^11][^12]

### Government / Law & Regulation

**{High}** **Taiwan (FSC) — Virtual Asset Service Act Advancing; Stablecoin Launch Targeted H2 2026** — Financial Supervisory Commission Chair **Peng Jin-long confirmed Cabinet review (early December 2025)** of Virtual Asset Service Act. Bill targets **Q2 2026 Executive Yuan submission**; first Taiwan-issued stablecoin launch **H2 2026 at earliest**. Rules mandate **full reserve backing, timely redemptions, initial issuance limited to financial institutions**. Penalties: up to NT\$5M fines + 2 years imprisonment for fraud/unlicensed ops.[^27][^28]

**{High}** **U.S. (SEC/CFTC) — Joint Regulatory Harmonization; CFTC Spot Crypto Trading Green Light (Dec 4)** — CFTC announced (Acting Chair Caroline Pham, Dec 4) that **spot crypto products will trade on federally registered futures exchanges for first time**. September SEC/CFTC joint statement clarified registered exchanges NOT prohibited from certain spot crypto commodity products. SEC Chair Atkins (Dec 2) indicated **"innovation exception" reveal in Jan 2026 or shortly after** (delayed by government shutdown).[^29][^18]

**{Medium}** **U.S. Senate (Boozman–Booker Draft, Released November)** — Bipartisan CFTC digital commodity authority bill proposes **exclusive CFTC jurisdiction over spot digital commodity markets** with new registration regimes for brokers, dealers, custodians, trading facilities. Core obligations: asset segregation, conflicts-of-interest safeguards, cybersecurity/operational resilience, governance, financial resources.[^18]

**{Medium}** **Singapore (MAS) — Tokenized Capital Markets Products Guide (Dec 15, 2025)** — Monetary Authority issued **revised Guide on Tokenisation of Capital Markets Products**, replacing earlier Digital Token Offerings guide. Clarifies DLT-based tokenization of traditional capital markets; encourages Token Assessments with legal opinion from SG-qualified lawyer.[^30]

**{Medium}** **EU/MiCA Implementation — Ongoing** — No new enforcement announcements Dec 13–20; MiCA enforcement framework active. Bitcoin MiCA whitepaper (PoC) published March 2024.[^31]

**No material actions from UK FCA, Hong Kong SFC/HKMA, Japan FSA in 72h window.**
