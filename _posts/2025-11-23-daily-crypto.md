### Top Headlines (7 Items)

**1. Cardano Network Recovers After Targeted Chain Split | Nov 22 (UTC) | {High}**

Cardano mainnet experienced a **rare chain partition on Nov 20–21** after a malformed delegation transaction exploited a deserialization bug in a 2022-vintage cryptographic library. The network split into two chains: one "poisoned," one "healthy." Nodes running version 10.5.x accepted the malformed tx; older versions rejected it. Within **~12 hours**, the healthy chain re-established consensus after operators upgraded to v10.5.3. **No user funds lost**; the identified wallet has ties to a former Incentivized Testnet participant. The **FBI has been contacted** to investigate as a possible cyberattack. Charles Hoskinson stated: *"The network survived. It didn't stop."* Intersect (the ecosystem steward) announced a comprehensive After-Action Review (AAR). **Primary source:** Intersect announcement; **secondary:** Crypto Briefing, CoinTrust.[^11][^12][^13]

**2. Bitcoin ETF Outflows Hit Third-Largest Weekly Withdrawal; IBIT Sheds \$1.6B | Nov 23 (UTC) | {High}**

Spot Bitcoin  ETFs faced **\$1.2B redemptions over the past week** — the third-largest weekly outflow since launch (22 months ago). Cumulative November outflows now stand at **\$3.79B**, matching the Feb 2025 record. BlackRock's IBIT alone lost **\$1.6B** from late Oct–mid Nov, with a single-day record outflow of **>\$900M on Thursday Nov 21**. Friday Nov 22 saw a partial reversal: FBTC rebounded with **\$108M inflows** (largest daily inflow for the category), and spot Bitcoin briefly recovered to \$86.1K. Analysts cite **macroeconomic headwinds** (Fed rate-cut hopes fading, AI valuation concerns) and a **transition from structural demand to neutral/negative ETF flow regime**. **Primary source:** Farside Investors (Bloomberg-affiliated); **secondary:** Yahoo Finance.[^14][^5]

**3. First US XRP Spot ETF Launches with Record Inflows; XRP Near Support | Nov 23 (UTC) | {Medium}**

Canary Capital launched the first US spot **Ripple (XRP)** ETF under ticker **XRPC**, recording **\$58M in opening-day inflows** — the **highest debut total for any ETF in 2025**. XRP is now trading at **\$2.0425 (+6.93% in 24h)**, though it hit a **7-week low of \$1.81** mid-week amid the broader correction. XRP ETFs have accrued **~\$500M cumulative inflows**. On Friday Nov 22, XRP inflows reached **\$118M**, signaling continued institutional demand. Technically, XRP sits on major support; analysts expect a rebound toward **\$2.70 if support holds**, else deeper correction to \$1.80. **Grayscale XRP ETF** and **Grayscale Dogecoin ETF** approved for listing on NYSE Arca, launching **Monday Nov 25**. **Primary source:** Yahoo Finance; **secondary:** The Block, Bankless Times.[^15][^2][^14]

**4. Solana, TON Spot ETFs Outpace Bitcoin; BSOL Amasses \$660M in 3 Weeks | Nov 23 (UTC) | {Medium}**

While Bitcoin ETFs bled capital, **Solana** and **Toncoin** spot ETFs attracted significant inflows. **Bitwise Solana ETF (BSOL)** accumulated **\$660M in assets within three weeks** with **zero outflow days**, vastly outpacing the XRP debut. This reflects a **rotation into lower-cap alts as retail re-engages**. Solana's daily active addresses recently surpassed Bitcoin and Ethereum, per Glassnode/Gemini 2025 Trends Report. TON gained institutional backing after Coinbase launched **TON-USD trading on Nov 18** (limit-only mode, then auction). Telegram's ecosystem of 900M+ users and corporate treasuries accumulating TON (e.g., TON Strategy Co., ~\$558M allocation) are cited as supply-constraining catalysts. **Primary source:** Yahoo Finance, FinanceMagnates.[^16][^17][^18][^14]

**5. SEC "Project Crypto" Advances: Token Taxonomy & "Regulation Crypto" in 2026 | Nov 16 (disclosed Nov 22) | {High}**

SEC Chairman **Paul Atkins** outlined the **next phase of Project Crypto** on Nov 12, signaling **formal rulemaking in 2026**. Key initiatives: (1) **Token taxonomy** clarifying when assets are securities, stablecoins, commodities, or collectibles; (2) **"Regulation Crypto"** proposal with tailored disclosures, exemptions, and safe harbors for token sales, airdrops, and network rewards; (3) **SEC-CFTC coordination** to streamline crypto derivative and spot trading rules; (4) **"super-app" framework** enabling broker-dealers to offer integrated crypto/equity/commodity services. OIRA signaled SEC formal rule proposals coming in **Q1–Q2 2026** for both a comprehensive crypto asset framework and amendments to the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 to accommodate crypto trading on registered exchanges. **No enforcement shifts announced yet** for ongoing litigation (e.g., Ripple/SEC); however, **regulatory clarity is now a near-term catalyst**. **Primary source:** Sidley Austin (summary of SEC remarks).[^19]

**6. Kraken Valued at \$20B in \$800M Funding Round; Expands US Derivatives Suite | Nov 18 | {Medium}**

Crypto exchange **Kraken** closed an **\$800M dual-tranche funding round** at a **\$20B valuation** — a **33% increase in under two months**. Lead investors include **Jane Street, Citadel Securities** (\$200M strategic tranche), **Tribe Capital**, and **Oppenheimer Alternative Investment**. The financing reflects **investor confidence in a pro-crypto regulatory environment** under the incoming U.S. administration. Kraken has also acquired **Small Exchange** (IG Group's futures platform) for **\$100M**, enabling a **fully US-based derivatives suite launch**. This positions Kraken as a **contender against Deribit** (currently dominant in crypto derivatives). **Primary source:** Economic Times, citing official Kraken announcement.[^20]

**7. Stablecoin Expansion Accelerates; RLUSD Crosses \$1.1B Supply, USDT Dominance Intact | Nov 20–22 | {Low}**

**Ripple's RLUSD** stablecoin supply reached **\$1.14B (with \$1.19B in reserves)** by Nov 20, growing **1,278% YTD** and surpassing **\$500M circulation within 7 months of July 2025 launch**. RLUSD is backed 1:1 by cash, Treasury bills, and liquid assets, supported on both XRP Ledger and Ethereum. Meanwhile, **USDT maintains dominance** with **\$175B circulating supply** (Q3 2025), steady institutional flows **\$30–50B/week**, and conservative expansion. **USDC** faces headwinds: supply fell to **~\$53–73B range in 2025**, with **\$9B+ weekly burns in September** suggesting strategic tightening after Q2 market pressure. **Institutional adoption of stablecoins as settlement infrastructure** is accelerating across Ethereum, Tron, Solana, and emerging L2s. **Primary source:** KuCoin, CrystalIntelligence, Coinlaw.[^9][^10][^21]
