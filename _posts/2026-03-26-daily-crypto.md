## Top Headlines (What & Why It Matters)

- **CLARITY Act stablecoin yield ban draft** – New draft language for the U.S. Digital Asset Market CLARITY Act would bar digital asset service providers and affiliates from paying any interest or yield “solely in connection with holding” payment stablecoins, closing a loophole left by the GENIUS Act that only restricted issuers. Commentators note this directly targets products like exchange “rewards” on USDC/USDT and reflects heavy bank lobbying; Coinbase and others have already opposed earlier versions on similar grounds. Risk: High (U.S. stablecoin yields, CeFi business models).[^6][^33][^34][^35][^36]
- **CFTC preparing onshore crypto perpetual futures framework** – CFTC Chair Michael Selig reiterated in early March that the agency is “working towards getting true perpetual futures … here in the U.S. within the next month or so,” with accompanying guidance on prediction markets, aiming to repatriate liquidity now concentrated on offshore venues. This would create a regulated home for perps alongside CME‑style futures, potentially reshaping where leverage resides. Risk: Medium.[^37][^7][^8][^38]
- **Bitcoin ETF flows re‑accelerate** – After weeks of choppy flows, U.S. spot BTC ETFs recorded net inflows of ~198M on March 17 and ~167M on March 23, with BlackRock’s IBIT and Fidelity’s FBTC leading while some peers saw flat or negative flows. ETFs now hold roughly 90B of BTC exposure, representing a material share of free‑float and acting as both a support and a concentration risk factor. Risk: Medium.[^25][^26][^24]
- **USDC and stablecoin market expansion** – Official‑data summaries show USDC’s circulating supply rose to about 79.1B as of March 19, backed by 79.4B in reserves (reverse repos, short‑term Treasuries, and bank deposits), while broader reporting pegs total stablecoin supply above 300B. This cements stablecoins as a primary liquidity rail even as U.S. lawmakers debate yield bans. Risk: Medium.[^30][^39][^12][^32][^27]
- **Chainalysis 2026 Crypto Crime Report** – Chainalysis estimates illicit addresses received at least 154B in 2025, a 162% year‑on‑year increase, driven by roughly 104B sent to sanctioned entities, a 694% surge. The report highlights DPRK‑linked actors, state‑level sanctions evasion, and evolving DeFi exploits and personal‑wallet scams as key trends, raising the bar for compliance and law‑enforcement scrutiny. Risk: Medium–High.[^40][^41][^42][^10][^43]
- **Mining margins and AI pivot** – CoinShares data summarized this week show public BTC miners’ weighted‑average cash cost near 79,995 per BTC in Q4 2025, with hashprice sliding toward 29 per PH/s/day in Q1 2026, prompting some miners to fund AI‑infrastructure pivots using debt and BTC sales. That combination of tight margins and balance‑sheet reallocation is structurally bearish if sustained. Risk: Medium.[^44]

## Regulation / Law

- **U.S. stablecoin regime – CLARITY vs. GENIUS**: Legal commentary confirms the Senate draft of the CLARITY Act adds Section 404, banning yield on payment stablecoins paid by “digital asset service providers” and affiliates, closing a gap left by the GENIUS Act that only barred issuers from paying interest. Industry and policy analysis frames this as a bank‑friendly compromise that could force exchanges and fintechs to redesign rewards products or shift them offshore. Risk: High.[^33][^34][^35][^36][^6]
- **CFTC perps guidance timeline**: Multiple reputable outlets quoting CFTC Chair Selig report that rules for “true” crypto perpetuals are targeted for release “within the next month or so,” alongside separate guidance on prediction markets. This is progressing even while broader market‑structure legislation stalls, signaling the derivatives regulator is acting without waiting for Congress. Risk: Medium.[^7][^8][^38][^37]
- **Other priority jurisdictions**: No material new crypto policy actions in the last 7 days were identified from MAS (Singapore), SFC/HKMA (Hong Kong), FSA (Japan), or Taiwan FSC; most current work continues to revolve around previously announced stablecoin and virtual‑asset licensing frameworks scheduled toward 2026 implementation. Risk: Low (near‑term).[^54][^55][^56][^57][^58]

## Security Incidents

- **Resolv (USR) exploit via compromised signing key** – A detailed postmortem shows the Resolv yield‑bearing stablecoin protocol was exploited after an attacker obtained an AWS KMS‑backed key controlling a privileged SERVICE_ROLE, allowing them to mint 80M USR against only ~100–200k USDC deposited. The attacker converted USR to wstUSR and then to ETH via Curve, Uniswap, and Kyber, ending with about 11,400 ETH (~24–25M), while core BTC/ETH collateral pools remained solvent; the vulnerability had been flagged in prior audits as a missing mint cap. Risk: High (DeFi governance and key management).[^3]
- **DeFi hacks >137M in 2026 YTD** – Aggregated data compiled March 23 indicate roughly 137M has been stolen across 15 DeFi protocols so far this year, with Step Finance (27.3M, key compromise), Truebit (26.2M, contract bug), Resolv (>25M, minting flaw), SwapNet (13.4M, arbitrary call), YieldBlox (~11M, oracle manipulation), and several others each losing 3.7–7M; only about 9M has been recovered, implying a ~6.5% recovery rate. Risk: High (sector‑wide).[^4][^5]
- **Step Finance shutdown after prior hack** – Step Finance, a major Solana DeFi dashboard, announced it will cease operations following a ~26M treasury hack disclosed in late January, stating that attempts at recapitalization or acquisition failed and that redemptions are being planned. This underscores how even non‑AMM, dashboard‑style projects can face existential risk from treasury key compromise. Risk: Medium.[^61]
