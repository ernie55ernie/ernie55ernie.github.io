**2. Aerodrome Finance (Base DEX) Suffers DNS Hijacking; >\$1M Drained, Smart Contracts Safe** üî¥ **{High}**
On 22 Nov (~06:00 UTC, 22 Nov in Asia/Singapore), Aerodrome Finance‚Äîthe leading DEX on the Base network‚Äîdetected a **DNS hijacking incident** compromising its centralized domains (.finance and .box), enabling attackers to execute phishing attacks siphoning ETH, USDC, NFTs via unlimited approval prompts. Over **\$1 million was stolen in under one hour**. The team confirmed all **smart contracts remain secure**; only the frontend was compromised. Aerodrome immediately disabled access to main URLs and set up two **decentralized mirrors** (aero.drome.eth.limo and aero.drome.eth.link) hosted on ENS infrastructure. Sister protocol **Velodrome Finance** reported a parallel attack, suggesting systematic targeting of Box Domains infrastructure. One affected developer reported recovering ~10‚Äì20% of funds via on-chain stealth operations using Jito bundles over three days.[^12][^13]

**Security Alert:** Users are advised to avoid all Aerodrome centralized domains and use only ENS-hosted mirrors until full domain recovery.

**Sources:** TradingView/Aerodrome official (22 Nov 03:03 UTC); Yahoo Finance (22 Nov)[^13][^12]

**3. SEC Project Crypto Advances: Token Taxonomy & "Regulation Crypto" Proposal on Horizon for 2026** ‚ö†Ô∏è **{Medium}**
On 12 Nov, SEC Chair Paul Atkins outlined the next phase of Project Crypto during remarks at an undisclosed industry event, signaling formal rulemaking incoming for 2026. Key initiatives include: (a) a **formal token taxonomy** clarifying asset classification (securities, stablecoins, digital commodities, collectibles); (b) refined **Howey framework** application for crypto offerings; (c) a forthcoming **"Regulation Crypto" proposal** with tailored disclosures, exemptions, and safe harbors for token sales, airdrops, and network rewards. Proposed innovations include allowing tokens tied to investment contracts to trade on non-SEC platforms (CFTC-registered or state-regulated). The SEC Division of Corporate Finance has already released five guidance statements on crypto securities compliance; formal rule proposals are expected by late 2025 or early 2026.[^14]

**5. Taiwan FSC Enforces Strict Crypto AML Rules; 26 Licensed Providers Must Register or Face Up to 2 Years Jail** ‚ö†Ô∏è **{Medium}**
Taiwan's Financial Supervisory Commission (FSC) is implementing **strict anti-money laundering (AML) regulations** for virtual asset service providers (VASPs) effective **30 Nov 2025**‚Äîmoved forward one month from the original 1 Jan 2025 deadline. All **26 licensed crypto providers** in Taiwan, whether previously registered or not, must comply. Requirements include monitoring customer names, bank accounts, IP locations, transaction patterns, and flagging suspicious activities (frequent account info changes, fund-splitting, same-IP multi-accounts). Non-compliance penalties: fines up to **NT\$5 million (~\$153,700 USD) and up to 2 years imprisonment**. The FSC has also issued a compliance checklist. Earlier in Nov, the regulator fined local exchanges **MaiCoin and BitoPro NT\$1.5 million each** for AML violations.[^18]

**Sources:** Crypto Potato (24 Nov 2024 reporting; current deadline Nov 30)[^18]
