## Regulation / Law

- **FATF targeted report on stablecoins and unhosted wallets (Risk: High for stablecoin ecosystem).**
  - On 3 March 2026, the Financial Action Task Force released a targeted report on stablecoins and peer‑to‑peer unhosted wallet transactions, highlighting that stablecoins have become the dominant medium in illicit virtual‑asset flows (estimated 84% of 2025 illicit volume) and calling out particular vulnerabilities in P2P transfers and cross‑chain activity.[^23][^24][^25]
  - The report urges countries to fully implement Recommendation 15 for stablecoin arrangements, impose AML/CFT obligations on issuers and intermediaries, and consider “programmable controls” (freeze, burn, deny‑lists/allow‑lists) at the smart‑contract level, which could materially affect design and operation of centralized stablecoins and DeFi integrations.[^26][^27][^23]

- **SEC Crypto Task Force takes written input on tokenization and solvency proofs (Risk: Medium).**
  - The SEC’s Crypto Task Force page shows new written submissions dated 5, 10, and 12 March 2026, including proposals for on‑chain proof‑of‑solvency frameworks and standardized tokenized‑security compliance interfaces (e.g., ERC‑7943) that embed transfer controls and freezing powers directly in tokens.[^28]
  - These inputs do not yet constitute rulemaking but signal areas where U.S. regulators may later formalize guidance, particularly around custody, real‑time solvency evidence, and compliance features of tokenized instruments.[^28]

- **MiCA implementation pressure intensifies in the EU (Risk: Medium).**
  - A recent practitioner analysis summarizing ESMA and EBA actions notes that as of 12 March 2026 there are 19 authorized e‑money‑token (EMT) issuers covering 29 tokens under MiCA, with the PSD2–MiCA enforcement convergence on 2 March raising compliance costs for CASPs that must now obtain dual licensing for payment services and crypto‑asset services in many cases.[^29]
