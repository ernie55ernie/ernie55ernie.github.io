## Top Headlines (What & Why it matters)

- **US spot BTC ETFs log ~962M USD net inflow on March 2, led by IBIT.** Multiple trackers report US spot BTC ETFs saw about 962.5M in net inflows yesterday, with BlackRock’s IBIT taking ~767.5M, Fidelity’s FBTC ~94.8M, and Bitwise’s BITB ~36.4M, with smaller positive flows into HODL, EZBC, BTCO, and ARKB. This reverses late‑February outflows and reinforces the ETF‑flow narrative behind BTC’s push back toward 70k. **Risk: High** (flows are currently the main marginal driver for BTC).[^20][^21][^22]
- **Ethereum Foundation publishes official “L1 Strawmap” roadmap site.** EF Architecture has launched strawmap.org as a living draft of Ethereum’s L1 roadmap through roughly 2029, organized around seven expected hard forks and five “north stars”: fast L1 finality (seconds), ~10,000 TPS “gigagas” L1 via zkEVMs, “teragas” L2 scaling, post‑quantum security, and native privacy (shielded ETH transfers). Justin Drake and Vitalik Buterin have amplified the plan on X, including proposals to gradually cut slot times from 12s toward 2s using a square‑root‑of‑two schedule. **Risk: Medium–High** (shapes multi‑year ETH thesis, L2 and MEV design).[^23][^4][^24][^25][^26]
- **Coinbase’s 2026 Crypto Market Outlook doubles down on institutionalization and “DAT 2.0”.** Coinbase’s official 2026 Crypto Market Outlook frames 2026 as an inflection where clearer regulation and accelerating institutional integration push crypto from speculation toward core financial infrastructure. The report highlights “DAT 2.0” (data‑availability and blockspace markets), the dominance of perps in market structure, and stablecoins/payment rails as key secular drivers rather than meme‑narrative cycles. **Risk: Medium** (supports longer‑horizon institutional flows and infra valuations).[^27][^28][^29][^30]
- **February hacks hit multi‑year low even as a few high‑profile DeFi exploits persist.** CertiK‑based data cited by Yahoo shows only about 35.7M stolen in February 2026 (down over 90% year‑on‑year), with 12 recorded incidents led by a ~10.9M oracle manipulation on Stellar’s YieldBlox Blend pool, a ~9M IoTeX bridge key compromise (see Security section), and a ~2.2M exploit of privacy protocol Foom.Cash. **Risk: Medium** (headline risk from individual exploits, but macro security trend improving).[^5]
- **Macro/Geopolitics: BTC resilience amid war headlines.** KuCoin and other outlets note that BTC has regained ground toward 68–70k despite ongoing geopolitical tensions and prior funding stress, with some analysts characterizing March as historically positive for BTC and ETH returns. **Risk: Medium** (macro or escalation could quickly flip risk sentiment).[^2][^31][^32]

## Regulation/Law

- **US stablecoin “Clarity Act” misses March 1 White House deadline.** Reporting indicates the White House’s March 1 deadline for a bipartisan stablecoin deal has lapsed without agreement, as banks and crypto firms clash over who can pay yield on stablecoins and how those products should be supervised. Negotiations now point to Senate Banking markup in mid‑to‑late March and another soft deadline around July before election‑year gridlock; absent a deal, regulators may lean more heavily on enforcement and guidance (SEC/OCC) to police yield‑bearing stablecoin products. **Risk: High** (directly affects USDC/USDT competitive landscape, bank‑token projects, and ETF‑like yield wrappers).[^41]
- **SEC Crypto Task Force continues to solicit technical input, including ZK privacy models.** The SEC’s Crypto Task Force “written input” page shows recent March 1 submissions specific to “programmable privacy & tiered disclosure with ZKPs,” arguing for regulator‑auditable privacy layers using zero‑knowledge proofs. While not yet policy, it signals active consideration of ZK‑based compliance architectures rather than pure surveillance or bans. **Risk: Medium** (shapes feasibility of privacy‑preserving DeFi and exchange designs in the US).[^42]

## Research/White Papers

- **Ethereum L1 Strawmap (EF Architecture, March 2 2026).** The official strawmap.org site lays out a work‑in‑progress but concrete visual roadmap for consensus, data, and execution‑layer upgrades through 2029, assuming roughly seven forks and five long‑term targets: sub‑second/seconds‑level finality, ~10k TPS L1, ~10M TPS L2 via data‑availability sampling, post‑quantum cryptography, and native privacy. This becomes the main reference for L1‑oriented ETH valuation and for L2 builders aligning roadmaps. **Risk: High** (core to ETH’s technical and economic trajectory).[^4][^24][^23]
- **Chainalysis 2026 Crypto Crime Report (Feb 13 2026).** Chainalysis’ latest crime report finds roughly 3.4B stolen in 2025, with North Korean actors responsible for ~2.02B and a shift toward larger centralized‑service compromises and personal wallet attacks even as DeFi hack losses stay suppressed relative to 2020–22, despite TVL recovery. It emphasizes faster, multi‑phase laundering patterns (DeFi → mixers → CEX) and highlights improved incident response in some protocols (e.g., Venus example with full recovery). **Risk: Medium** (affects insurance pricing, compliance, and regulator posture).[^43][^44][^45]
- **Coinbase 2026 Crypto Market Outlook (institutional research).** Coinbase’s institutional research desk argues that 2026 will look more like “1996 than 1999,” with structural growth driven by derivatives‑dominant market structure, prediction markets, and stablecoins as payment rails, under a gradually clearer regulatory regime. It expects token economics to shift toward cash‑flow‑backed models (fee‑share, buyback/burn) and highlights ZKP/FHE‑based privacy as a prerequisite for deeper institutional adoption. **Risk: Medium** (influences institutional positioning and sector tilts).[^28][^29][^30][^27]

## Security Incidents

- **IoTeX bridge exploit (~4–8M loss) via compromised validator key.** On February 21 (07:00–09:00 UTC window), IoTeX’s ioTube cross‑chain bridge suffered a private‑key compromise allowing an attacker to seize control of Ethereum‑side TokenSafe and MinterPool contracts, drain vault assets (USDC, USDT, IOTX, WBTC, BUSD, etc.), and mint additional CIOTX and CCS tokens; estimates of realized loss range from about 4.3M to over 8M as analysts factor in over‑minted tokens. IoTeX paused bridge operations, coordinated with exchanges to freeze deposits, and temporarily halted main‑chain activity while hardening key management; investigations suggest overlap with a prior 49M exploit on another stablecoin platform. **Risk: High** (bridge risk; cross‑chain collateral and any IoTeX‑linked DeFi positions).[^46][^47][^6]
- **Step Finance (Solana) winds down after 26M treasury hack.** Step Finance, once branded the “front page of Solana,” announced on February 23 that it will cease all operations—including affiliated SolanaFloor and Remora Markets—after failing to secure financing or acquisition options following a January 31 treasury‑wallet compromise estimated around 26M. The team is working on STEP token buybacks (snapshot‑based) and redemptions for Remora rTokens, but the shutdown underscores how one large treasury hack can be terminal for infra‑heavy DeFi projects even when core chains (Solana) remain healthy. **Risk: Medium–High** (Solana DeFi reputational risk; concentrated protocol‑treasury exposures).[^48][^3]
- **Macro security context: DeFi exploits lower, but bridge/oracle risks remain focal.** February’s security summary (CertiK/Yahoo) shows only ~35.7M lost across 12 incidents (oracle manipulation, bridge bugs, cryptographic edge cases, phishing), with IoTeX, YieldBlox Blend (~10.86M oracle attack on Stellar), and Foom.Cash (~2.2M) among the largest. Analysts highlight a shift from rampant protocol‑TVL rug‑pulls toward key‑management failures, oracle design flaws, and personal‑wallet compromises. **Risk: Medium** (tail risk persists, but systemic DeFi‑TVL drain risk currently subdued).[^49][^50][^5]
