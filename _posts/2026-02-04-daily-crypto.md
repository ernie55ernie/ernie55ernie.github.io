**2. SEC & CFTC Announce Joint "Project Crypto" – Historic Regulatory Unification {HIGH}**

- Jan 29–30, 2026: SEC Chair Paul Atkins & CFTC Chair Michael Selig announced unified digital-asset oversight replacing parallel SEC-only Project Crypto[^11][^12]
- **Core goals**: Eliminate duplicative compliance, develop shared token taxonomy (securities vs. commodities), clarify jurisdictional boundaries[^12]
- **Modernization**: Rulemaking for tokenized collateral, responsible leverage products, perpetual contracts (which developed offshore due to prior regulatory uncertainty)[^12]
- **Atkins quote**: "One of the most ambitious collaborations between our two agencies in a generation"[^12]
- **Why it matters**: Signals institutional-ready regulatory clarity; removes key friction for TradFi crypto adoption. **Medium-term bullish for infrastructure.**
- **Primary source**: SEC & CFTC official speeches (Jan 29–30, 2026) and legal memo[^11][^12]

**3. SEC Removes Crypto From 2026 Examination Priorities – Regulatory Stance Shift {MEDIUM}**

- SEC Division of Examinations published 2026 priorities (Nov 17, 2025) with **no explicit crypto or digital asset focus**[^13]
- **Contrast**: 2025 priorities (under Gary Gensler) explicitly named spot Bitcoin & Ethereum ETFs and crypto advisory services for close monitoring[^13]
- **Atkins (new chair) follow-up**: Rescinded Staff Accounting Bulletin 121 (which treated crypto holdings as balance-sheet liabilities); issued guidance that most meme coins are NOT securities[^13]
- **Why it matters**: Reflects Trump administration's pro-crypto stance; reduced SEC enforcement scrutiny expected to ease institutional hesitation. **Short-term positive sentiment**, though long-term clarity still pending.
- **Primary source**: SEC Division of Examinations official notice; SEC chair statements[^13]

**4. Stablecoin Transaction Volume Hits Record \$33 Trillion in 2025 – Up 72% YoY {MEDIUM}**

**5. Tether Launches USAT – Federally Regulated US Stablecoin Under GENIUS Act {MEDIUM}**

- **Why it matters**: Supply-chain wallet attacks now sophisticated (nation-state-level persistence); institutional self-custody risk highlighted; users re-evaluating CEX vs. self-custodial tradeoffs.
