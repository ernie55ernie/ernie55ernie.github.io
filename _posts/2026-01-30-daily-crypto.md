### **MARKET OVERVIEW**

Bitcoin slid to a two-month low overnight, dropping **6.16%** to **\$82,686 USD** amid broad liquidations tied to escalating Iran–US geopolitical tensions. Ethereum followed with a **6.93% decline**, touching **\$2,687 on Binance**—a similar two-month trough. Across the top 10, Solana underperformed with a **7.7% drop**, while BNB and XRP posted modest gains (+2.47%, +1.36% respectively). Total crypto market capitalization fell **6%** in the past 24 hours, with global spot trading volume declining **3.52%**.[^1][^2][^3][^4]

**Liquidations dominated the session:** A cascading **\$1.7 billion in leveraged positions were liquidated**, with longs accounting for **\$1.57 billion** of that tally. Bitcoin bore the heaviest load at **\$768.69 million** (97% from long positions), while Ethereum liquidations hit **\$417.43 million**. Hyperliquid and Bybit led the volume, each processing over \$300 million in liquidations. Funding rates remain **elevated and positive** across major perpetual exchanges, signaling persistent long bias despite downside pressure.[^2][^5][^6]

Stablecoin dynamics show early warning signs. **Total stablecoin supply sits at ~\$303 billion**—down from late-2025 highs—with USDT (≈\$184B) and USDC (≈\$76–78B) dominating 80%+ of the market. Tether's recent **3-billion USDT burn**, its largest since May 2025, signals significant capital outflows. The 60-day moving average of USDT market cap growth collapsed to **\$3.3 billion** from **\$15 billion** six weeks prior, a bearish lead indicator historically correlated with Bitcoin downside.[^7][^8]

Derivatives open interest stands at **\$98.7 billion** with moderate liquidation exposure clustering around **\$738 million ETH long exposure at \$2,665 support** and **\$1.29 billion in short exposure near \$2,941 resistance**.[^9]
