### **Top Headlines (What & Why It Matters)**

**1. ETF Inflows Resume, But Price Pressure Persists | Low Risk**

- U.S. spot Bitcoin and Ethereum ETFs recorded their first November inflows on Nov 6 (\$240M BTC; \$12.5M ETH) after \$2.9B six-day outflow streak (Oct 29–Nov 5). BlackRock's IBIT and ETHA led the rally. Interpretation: institutional interest stabilizing, but sustained price weakness—despite inflows—suggests sideways consolidation ahead of macro clarity.[^17][^8][^7]
- **Primary source**: BeInCrypto; **Secondary**: Farside Investors, CoinDesk.[^17]

**2. Balancer DeFi Protocol Exploited for \$120M+ | High Risk**

- On Nov 2, hackers compromised the Balancer protocol via a rounding error in batched swap logic (EXACT_OUT function). Attack method: exploited decimal-scaling mismatches—upscaling to 18-decimals rounded DOWN, downscaling rounded UP, accumulating tiny imbalances over repeated trades. Pools paused; Berachain deployed emergency hard fork. Chainalysis warned DeFi sector faces "serious security vulnerabilities." **Note**: Balancer had 10 prior audits by top firms, highlighting audit fatigue and AI-crafted exploit sophistication.[^18][^19]
- **Primary source**: The Record; **Secondary**: Gizmodo, CoinDesk.

**3. XRP ETF Catalysts Accelerate—Dual Filings Enter 20-Day Window | Medium Risk**

Bloomberg ETF analyst Eric Balchunas noted dual filings could force SEC's first XRP-ETF decision—precedent set by approved BTC/ETH products. Ripple announced partnerships with Mastercard and WebBank for RLUSD settlement; Ripple President Monica Long said "no IPO timeline," bolstering focus on stablecoin/enterprise adoption. XRP newly created 21,595 wallets (48h; highest in 8 months).[^20][^21][^11]
- **Primary source**: CoinDesk; **Secondary**: Trading View, Bloomberg (via CoinDesk).
