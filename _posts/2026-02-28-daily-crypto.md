Changelly’s XRP and SOL dashboards similarly show F&G at 13 (“Extreme Fear”), reinforcing the risk‑off tone.[^26][^27][^28]


## Top Headlines (What & Why it matters)

- {Risk: High} Deep risk‑off flush with crowded shorts and heavy liquidations
– BTC is down ~3.2% 24h to 65.5k while ETH is down ~6%, and majors like SOL, AVAX, ADA, DOGE and SUI show 4–10% daily losses, with 86% of tracked coins negative.[^4][^8][^9][^10][^14][^2]
– Perpetual funding on BTC hit about ‑6% as price traded near 63k after reported U.S./Israeli strikes on Iran, with BTC OI increasing and >500M in positions—mostly longs—liquidated, indicating aggressive shorting plus forced de‑risking.[^29][^30][^1]
- {Risk: Medium} BTC spot ETFs swing from strong inflows to small outflows
– After roughly 257–507M USD of net inflows on Feb 24–25 and another ~360M on Feb 26, U.S. spot BTC ETFs posted a 27.6M net outflow on Feb 27 (ET).[^18][^19][^20][^17]
– IBIT has dominated cumulative inflows (over 61.5B historically) but led yesterday’s net outflow (~32.7M), suggesting some profit‑taking even as aggregate ETF AUM remains high and structurally important to BTC demand.[^19][^17]
- {Risk: Medium} Stablecoin supply rotation: USDT slips, USDC edges up
– USDT market cap has declined from a 186.8B ATH to about 183.6B, with a 0.8% drop in February following a 1% decline in January, marking its first consecutive monthly contraction since the Terra era.[^22][^24]
– In contrast, USDC’s market cap has climbed from about 70B to ~75.2B in early 2026, and now stands near that level with daily trading volume over 7–11B, suggesting gradual rotation toward USDC even as USDT remains dominant.[^23][^25][^21]
- {Risk: Medium} Hong Kong SFC greenlights VA margin financing and perps for pros
– On Feb 11 (within the last 2 weeks), the SFC issued new guidance allowing licensed VA brokers to provide margin financing for virtual assets and setting a framework for SFC‑licensed virtual asset trading platforms (VATPs) to offer perpetual contracts and other leveraged VA products to professional investors.[^5][^31]
– Commentary from KPMG on Feb 26 emphasizes that this is a key step in Hong Kong’s ASPIRe roadmap, aiming to deepen VA liquidity while imposing strict risk‑management and disclosure expectations on intermediaries.[^32]
- {Risk: High} IoTeX ioTube bridge hack via compromised validator key (~4.4M USD)
– In February 2026, IoTeX’s ioTube cross‑chain bridge was exploited after a private key controlling the Validator contract on Ethereum was compromised and used to deploy a malicious contract upgrade, bypassing validation and signature checks and draining at least 4.4M USD in assets.[^3]
– The attacker gained control over TokenSafe and MintPool contracts, highlighting the systemic risk of admin‑key compromise on bridges and reinforcing industry pressure to harden key management and minimize upgrade trust.[^3]
- {Risk: Medium} Step Finance (Solana DeFi dashboard) shuts down weeks after ~40M treasury hack
– Step Finance reported that around 40M USD was drained from its treasury and fee wallets on Jan 31 (APAC afternoon), likely via compromised executive devices, with about 4.7M recovered through token‑specific safeguards and partner coordination.[^33]
– On Feb 23, coverage indicated the Solana DeFi dashboard is shutting down, citing unsustainable infra costs plus the hack’s impact, underlining consolidation risk for non‑core revenue DeFi tooling.[^34]

## Security Incidents

- {Risk: High} IoTeX ioTube bridge hack (~4.4M USD)
– IoTeX’s ioTube cross‑chain bridge was exploited after an attacker obtained the private key of the account owning the Validator contract on Ethereum, used it to push a malicious contract upgrade, bypass validation/signature checks, and drain at least 4.4M USD from TokenSafe and MintPool.[^3]
– The incident underscores persistent cross‑chain bridge risk, especially where upgradeable contracts and centralized key custody intersect; projects with similar validator‑key architectures should evaluate governance and key‑management controls.
- {Risk: Medium} CrossCurve bridge exploit (~3M USD; earlier in Feb but still relevant)
– On Jan 31–Feb 2, CrossCurve’s cross‑chain bridge was attacked via a fabricated message vulnerability in its Axelar‑based Receiver contract, allowing spoofed cross‑chain messages to trigger unauthorized token unlocks and drain about 3M USD.[^51][^52][^53][^54]
– CrossCurve has paused bridge operations and urged LPs to withdraw; the case is being widely cited in security write‑ups as another example of insufficient message‑validation in interoperability stacks.[^52][^51]
- {Risk: Medium} Step Finance Solana treasury compromise (~40M) and shutdown
– Step Finance disclosed that approximately 40M USD was drained from treasury wallets on Jan 31 via compromised executive devices, with partial recovery (~4.7M) through on‑chain controls on specific tokens.[^33]
– A Feb 23 article reports the platform is now shutting down and focusing on redemptions/buybacks, signaling both security and business‑model fragility for DeFi dashboards reliant on treasury revenues.[^34]
