### **Top Headlines**

- **Grayscale Files Spot BNB ETF** – Jan 22–23: Grayscale submitted an S-1 registration with the SEC for a spot BNB exchange-traded fund (ticker GBNB, Nasdaq listing), with Coinbase serving as custodian. VanEck is pursuing a parallel BNB ETF (VBNB), signaling institutional confidence in expanding crypto products beyond Bitcoin and Ether. Combined spot BTC/ETH ETFs now hold >\$100B in assets under management, validating demand for regulated access. **{Risk: Medium}**[^8][^9][^10]
- **Crypto Startups Secure \$362M Amid Geopolitical Uncertainty** – Jan 23: Despite macro headwinds, the sector attracted significant institutional funding, underscoring sustained confidence in digital asset infrastructure.[^11][^12]
- **UK FCA Advances Crypto Regulation** – Jan 16–23: The Financial Conduct Authority finalized three consultation papers (CP25/40 and companions) on crypto activities, prudential standards, and market abuse. The FCA announced it will open the cryptoasset application gateway in September 2026, with the full regulatory regime commencing October 25, 2027. Feedback deadline: February 12, 2026. This clarity accelerates institutional onboarding.[^13][^14][^15]
- **Russian Central Bank Eases Crypto Licensing Framework** – Jan 23 (announced Dec 23): Moscow unveiled a proposed two-tier regulatory framework legalizing spot crypto trading. Non-qualified investors may purchase up to 300,000 rubles (~\$3,300 USD) annually through licensed intermediaries; qualified investors face no volume caps (privacy coins excluded). Implementation begins July 1, 2026; enforcement penalties for unlicensed intermediaries commence July 1, 2027. This marks a significant pivot from Russia's prior ambiguous stance.[^16][^17]
- **Binance & Coinbase Simultaneously List Sentient (SENT)** – Jan 22: Binance added Sentient (SENT/USDT, SENT/USDC, SENT/TRY) to its spot market; Coinbase launched perpetual futures on the same day. The Ethereum-based AI token attracted simultaneous institutional venue exposure.[^18]
- **SEC–CFTC Harmonization Event – Jan 27**: Both agencies will hold a joint public event (10 AM–11 AM ET) to discuss regulatory coordination on crypto. SEC Chair Paul Atkins and CFTC Chair Michael Selig emphasized eliminating jurisdictional ambiguity.[^19]
