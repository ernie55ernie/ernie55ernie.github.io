### **Market Overview**

**Prices & Moves**

Bitcoin (BTC) trades at \$90,011–\$90,480, down 1.1–1.77% over 24h, holding within the \$90,000–\$92,000 range ahead of the FOMC decision (Dec 9–10). Ethereum (ETH) at \$3,122–\$3,123, down 0.3–1.39%. Top alts under pressure: Solana (SOL) −3.92%, XRP −2.21%, ADA −2.07%, BNB −2.47%, DOGE −2.51%. Total market cap: \$3.13–\$3.17 trillion, down 1.2% with 86 of top 100 coins negative.[^1][^2][^3][^4][^5][^6]

**Derivatives & Sentiment**

Fear index collapsed to 19 (Extreme Fear), down from ~21 prior. Funding rates remain negative across BTC and ETH perpetuals, signaling net short positioning despite recent recovery. Bitcoin ETF outflow of \$166 million on Dec 8 alone; cumulative outflow of \$177 million month-to-date. USDT and USDC remain primary liquidity corridors; stablecoin volumes steady (~\$1.25T adjusted monthly).[^7][^8][^9][^10][^11]

**Narrative**: Market "low-volume gains" in altcoins; macro headwinds (FOMC uncertainty, Fed labor-data delays, tariff concerns) dominate sentiment.

### **Top Headlines — What Matters Most**

**1. SEC Ends Ondo Finance Investigation Without Charges — {Low Impact, High Precedent}**[^12][^13][^14][^15]
Closes two-year probe (launched Oct 2023) into tokenized U.S. Treasuries and ONDO token compliance. Final notice issued late Nov 2025. Marks significant regulatory shift under new SEC leadership; clears path for tokenized real-world assets (RWAs) in the U.S. market. Ondo plans Ondo Summit (Feb 3, 2026, NYC) to unveil expanded roadmap.

**2. BlackRock Files S-1 for iShares Staked Ethereum Trust (ETHB) — {Low Market Impact, Strategic}**[^16][^17][^18]
S-1 filing submitted Dec 7. New product would offer ETH spot price + staking rewards. Separate from existing ETHA (\$13B+ AUM). Indicates BlackRock's push into yield-bearing ETH products; may compete with anticipated staking features on ETHA itself. Expected SEC decision timeline: Q4 2025–April 2026 per Bloomberg.

**3. SEC Announces Dec 15 Crypto Privacy Roundtable — {Medium Regulatory Risk}**[^19][^20][^21][^22]
Four-hour session at SEC HQ, Washington D.C., with privacy technologists, compliance pros, and regulators. Focus: how crypto firms handle data, surveillance tools, privacy-preserving tech (zero-knowledge proofs, selective disclosure), and AML/CFT alignment. Public webcast available. Outcomes may guide future U.S. privacy policy; SEC Crypto Task Force coordinating with CFTC for unified approach.

**4. Coinbase Lists PLUME and JUPITER for Spot Trading — {Low Direct Market Impact}**[^23][^24][^25][^26]
Trading pairs (PLUME-USD, JUPITER-USD) went live Dec 9, 9 AM PT (~5 PM UTC), pending sufficient liquidity. PLUME: full-stack RWA blockchain. JUPITER: Solana DEX aggregator. Early order books will signal institutional appetite for RWAs and Solana infrastructure tools.

**5. Bittensor (TAO) Halving Scheduled Dec 14 — {Medium Technical Impact}**[^27][^28][^29]
First halving cuts daily token issuance from 7,200 to 3,600 TAO. Inflation drops to ~13%; supply approaches 21M cap (Bitcoin-like model). Analysts predict TAO volatility and potential scarcity-driven rallies; key milestone for network maturation. Token up ~10% on announcement; watches for Dec 14 execution.

**6. Do Kwon Sentencing (Terraform Labs) — Dec 11 — {Low Market Direct Impact, Symbolic}**[^30][^31][^32]
U.S. prosecutors seek 12-year sentence; defense argues for 5 years. Hearing before Judge Paul Engelmayer (NYC Federal Court). \$40B Terra collapse (May 2022), fraud, wire conspiracy charges. Kwon pleaded guilty Aug 2025; agreed to forfeit \$19.3M. South Korea pursuing separate 40-year sentence. Sentencing outcome may set precedent for high-profile crypto fraud cases.

**7. Upbit Hack & Yearn Finance Exploit — {Medium Security Risk}**[^33][^34]
Yearn's yETH LST pool exploited Nov 30: ~1,000 ETH (\$3M) drained via minting vulnerability; $9M total loss. Attacker routed funds through Tornado Cash. Yearn V2/V3 vaults unaffected. Highlights DeFi infrastructure risks amid institutional inflows. Upbit (Korean exchange) also hacked (~$millions); underscores year-end security vigilance.

### **Government / Law & Regulation**

**U.S. (SEC, CFTC)**

- SEC Ondo investigation closure: No charges; tokenized assets pathway cleared.[^13][^14][^12]
- SEC Privacy Roundtable Dec 15; SEC/CFTC alignment on oversight framework.[^20][^21][^22][^19]
- CFTC clears spot crypto on regulated derivatives platforms (no date limit announced yet).[^35]

**Taiwan (FSC)**

- Virtual Assets Service Act nearing final legislative approval. Stablecoin rollout expected H2 2026 (6-month regulatory buffer after law enactment). Only licensed financial institutions permitted initially; full reserve backing, local custody mandated.[^36][^37]

**EU (MiCA)**

- Privacy coin ban effective July 2027 under MiCA framework; already driving delisting pressure (73 delistings globally in 2025). ZEC, DASH, DCR rallying partly as regulatory hedge.[^38][^39]

**Singapore (MAS)**

- Maintains dual-track regulatory posture; no new guidance 7-day window.

**Japan (FSA), Hong Kong (SFC)**

- No material updates 7-day window.

- November adjusted volume \$1.25T (up 27% YoY). USDT \$895B (55% of volume on Ethereum), USDC \$1.62T. Velocity rising for USDC/PYUSD; USDT showing lower velocity (long-term reserve bias). Bots (70% of transactions), remittances, and payroll flows blend.

**BlackRock Staking Strategy**[^17][^18][^16]

- Staking ETF potential \$10–20B inflows by mid-2026 per BlackRock head of digital assets. May lock meaningful ETH supply; implications for L2 liquidity and validator economics.

### **Security Incidents**

**Yearn Finance (yETH) Exploit — Nov 30**[^34][^33]

- **Amount**: \$9M protocol loss; \$3M ETH stolen + moved to Tornado Cash.
- **Root**: Unlimited minting vulnerability in yETH stableswap pool (Balancer integration).
- **Status**: Under investigation; V2/V3 vaults remain secure.
- **Risk Level**: Medium (non-core vault impact); underscores DeFi contract-level audit gaps.

**Upbit (Korean Exchange) Hack**[^33]

- Multi-million dollar compromise; details emerging. South Korean regulator response pending.

**Year-to-Date DeFi Incidents**: \$135M+ lost to DeFi exploits; \$29.8M via exchange hacks (mid-2025 data, CertiK). Balancer cross-chain exploit (Nov) remains largest single event (\$116M+).
