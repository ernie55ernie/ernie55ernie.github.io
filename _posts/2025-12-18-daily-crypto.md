### Top Headlines (Market-Moving Items)

**1. Bybit \$1.4B Hack Recovery Ongoing; \$300M+ Cashed Out by Lazarus Group**[^10][^11][^12][^13]
{Low to Medium} — The February 21, 2025 theft of 401,347 Ethereum remains the largest crypto heist on record. Forensic analysis by law enforcement and blockchain analysts now confirms that North Korea's Lazarus Group has already laundered and cashed out approximately \$300 million through complex bridge swaps, DEX aggregators, and privacy protocols. Bybit secured bridge loans covering ~80% of stolen assets and continues recovery efforts in coordination with international authorities. CEO Ben Zhou reaffirmed that all customer funds remain safe and properly backed.[^11][^12]

**2. 2025 Crypto Theft Reaches \$3.4B; DeFi Security Improves Despite Scale**[^14]
{Medium} — Chainalysis reports that cryptocurrency theft totaled \$3.4 billion in 2025, with centralized exchange hacks accounting for 88% of Q1 2025 losses alone. However, DeFi protocols recorded fewer hacks despite recovering total value locked, reflecting improved security infrastructure (e.g., Venus governance freeze of attacker assets in November 2025). DPRK-linked actors remain the primary threat.[^14]

**3. React Server Component Vulnerability (CVE-2025-55182) Fuels Automated Crypto Wallet Drainer Attacks**[^15][^16]
{High} — A critical React flaw disclosed December 3 is now being actively exploited (observed December 4+) to inject wallet-draining scripts into legitimate cryptocurrency websites. Attackers also deploy XMRig cryptocurrency miners. Security teams report 15+ distinct clusters engaging in intrusion activity. Patch urgency is classified as critical.[^16][^15]

**4. Bitcoin ETF Flows Reverse; \$457M Inflow on December 17 Offsets Weekly Losses**[^6][^7]
{Low} — Following two days of combined \$635 million in outflows (December 15–16), Bitcoin ETFs recorded a strong rebound with \$457 million net inflows on December 17. Fidelity's FBTC dominated with \$391 million, reaching \$12.4 billion in assets. However, weekly flows remain negative at −\$177 million.[^7][^6]

**5. EU MiCA Passport Expands; 53+ Licenses Issued, 92% of Trades Now Compliant**[^17][^18]
{Low} — The European Union's Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA), fully operational since December 30, 2024, has issued 53+ licenses enabling passporting across 30 EEA countries. Major exchanges (Binance, Kraken, Coinbase) secured compliance for all 27 EU member states. By Q3 2025, 92% of crypto trades processed through MiCA-compliant venues. Remaining ambiguity surrounds NFT classification (~45% of projects); EU regulators plan 2025 amendments.[^18][^17]

**6. CFTC Clears Path for Spot Crypto Trading on Federally Registered Exchanges**[^19]
{Medium} — On December 4, 2025, Acting CFTC Chair Caroline Pham announced that spot cryptocurrency products may now trade on federally registered futures exchanges. This follows a September 2025 SEC/CFTC joint statement clarifying that such venues are not prohibited from facilitating certain spot crypto commodities. The move aligns with the Trump administration's stated goal of establishing U.S. leadership in crypto innovation.[^19]

**7. Trump's December 17 Address to Nation Largely Bypasses Crypto; On-Chain Activity Suggests Risk-Off Positioning**[^20][^9]
{Low} — President Trump delivered a 9 PM EST nationwide address on December 17 focusing on domestic policy achievements but provided no material crypto commentary. Prediction market interest in crypto mentions reached only \$11,000 in volume compared to \$21,000 for general politics. On-chain data showed large Ethereum movements to Binance ahead of the speech, signaling potential de-risking.[^9][^20]

**8. OKX Announces Perpetual Futures Discontinuation \& Wallet Upgrade**[^21]
{Low} — OKX announced on December 17 the discontinuation of BTC/USDT- and ETH/USDT-margined expiry futures. The exchange also rolled out gas-free stablecoin transfers on X Layer via its OKX Wallet. Additionally, position tier adjustments for TRUTH/USDT perpetual were announced for December 18.[^21]
