## Market Overview (24h)

- **Spot & total market cap:** Global crypto market cap is roughly in the mid‑\$2T range and up low‑single digits over the last 24h, with Bitcoin around the high‑\$60k zone and Ethereum near the high‑\$1.9k level based on multiple real‑time and intraday snapshots. BTC has bounced sharply from weekend lows near the low‑\$60ks on geopolitical headlines, while ETH has lagged slightly but remains above key $~\$1.9k$ support. Among major alts, SOL is in the high‑\$80s, up about 1% over 24h, with broad top‑50 altcoins mostly +1–3% on the day. {Risk: Medium}[^5][^6][^7][^8][^9][^10][^11][^12]
- **Derivatives:** Aggregated BTC perpetual futures open interest across major venues is about **\$94.3B**, up ~3% over 24h, with roughly **\$378M** in 24h liquidations and a long/short split around **51% / 49%**, signaling moderate re‑risking after prior deleveraging. Venue breakdown shows Binance (~\$8.1B OI), CME (~\$7.3B), Bybit, OKX, and others also seeing small OI increases over the last 24h. Funding remains close to neutral on average (no persistent premium for longs or shorts), consistent with Glassnode’s recent on‑chain read that leverage has reset and options markets are cautious but not stressed. {Risk: Medium}[^13][^1]
- **Stablecoins & flows:** Recent research and policy work put total USD‑pegged stablecoin supply above **\$280B** at end‑2025 and over **\$300B** by early 2026, with USDT around \$175–185B and USDC about \$74–75B. CryptoQuant notes USDT’s market cap has shrunk by about **\$133M** over the last 60 days—the first sustained decline since late‑2023—supporting the view that stablecoin liquidity is no longer expanding and is mildly contractionary in the current bear phase. {Risk: Medium}[^14][^15][^16][^17]

## Top Headlines (What & Why it matters)

- **Spot BTC ETFs see \$458M net inflow, breaking outflow streak:** On **2026‑03‑02 (U.S. close, ~2026‑03‑03 02:30 UTC)**, U.S. spot BTC ETFs recorded **\$458.2M** in net inflows, the first positive day for March after a five‑week outflow trend totaling about \$4–4.5B. BlackRock’s IBIT led with **\$263M**, followed by Fidelity’s FBTC (~\$95M), Bitwise BITB (~\$36M), and others; no major product reported outflows that day, indicating a broad‑based institutional bid returning. {Risk: High}[^18][^3][^19][^20][^4][^21]
- **CFTC chair targets on‑shore crypto perpetual futures “within a month”:** CFTC Chairman Michael Selig said at the Milken Institute’s Future of Finance event in Washington on **2026‑03‑02 (~2026‑03‑02 19:00–21:00 UTC)** that the agency is “working towards getting true perpetual futures… here in the U.S. within the next month or so,” as part of its Project Crypto harmonization push with the SEC. He framed this as an effort to “recapture” liquidity that migrated offshore, signaling forthcoming guidance or rulemaking that could legitimize U.S.‑listed crypto perps under tighter leverage and transparency rules. {Risk: High}[^22][^23][^24][^25][^26][^27]
- **FATF issues new warning that stablecoins dominate illicit crypto activity:** On **2026‑03‑03 (~2026‑03‑03 12:00 UTC)** the Financial Action Task Force released a 42‑page report finding that stablecoins such as USDT now account for the bulk of illicit virtual‑asset transaction volume, including sanctions evasion tied to Iran and North Korea. Citing Chainalysis and TRM data, FATF notes stablecoins represented **84% of \$154B** in illicit crypto volume in 2025 and urges countries to require stablecoin issuers to implement tools like wallet‑freezing, burning, and potentially mandatory deny‑lists for secondary‑market addresses. {Risk: High}[^28][^29][^30][^31][^32]
- **Macro backdrop: BTC rebounds alongside ETF flows amid Iran–Hormuz shock:** As Iran announced closure of the Strait of Hormuz and regional tensions escalated, BTC briefly traded down toward the low‑\$60ks over the weekend before rebounding to the high‑\$60k range, tracking a V‑shaped recovery in U.S. equities. Daily market reports tie this rebound explicitly to the ETF inflow day on March 2 and view it as an important test of BTC’s “digital gold” narrative under acute geopolitical stress. {Risk: High}[^33][^6][^4][^34][^18]

## Regulation/Law

- **CFTC Project Crypto & perps framework:** Beyond the headline on perps timing, Selig reiterated under “Project Crypto” that the CFTC will use exemptive tools and new guidance to on‑shore perpetual futures and clarify treatment of prediction markets and DeFi, often in joint coordination with the SEC. Law‑firm summaries highlight that this comes on top of earlier CFTC no‑action/self‑certification steps that already allowed BTC and ETH perpetuals on U.S. DCMs, suggesting a broader extension to more assets and venues. {Risk: High}[^44][^45][^24][^26][^46][^27]
- **FATF stablecoin guidance:** FATF’s new report explicitly calls out stablecoins as “the most popular virtual asset used in illicit transactions” and urges jurisdictions to impose full AML/CFT obligations on issuers and VASPs, tighten rules for peer‑to‑peer transfers via unhosted wallets, and consider technical controls like freezing or burning tokens linked to sanctioned or high‑risk activity. Commentators expect MiCA in the EU and the U.S. GENIUS Act regime to be treated as early reference implementations for compliant payment stablecoins. {Risk: High}[^17][^31][^47][^48][^28]

## Research/White Papers

- **Glassnode “Week On‑chain – Waiting for Conviction” (2026‑02‑24):** Glassnode finds the 90‑day Realized Profit/Loss Ratio has fallen below 1.0, indicating an “excess loss regime” and structurally impaired liquidity similar to late‑stage prior bear markets. ETF flows are persistently negative, large‑holder accumulation has slowed, and funding is near neutral; historically, such conditions have correlated with markets closer to a bottoming range than an early‑cycle top, but with elevated downside risk if new demand fails to appear. {Risk: Medium}[^1]
- **Chainalysis 2026 Crypto Crime Report (Feb 2026 chapters):** Chainalysis estimates at least **\$154B** was received by illicit addresses in 2025, a **162% YoY** increase, with stablecoins making up **84%** of illicit volume and North Korean actors stealing about **\$2B**. A dedicated scams chapter estimates at least **\$14B** in on‑chain scam inflows in 2025, with projections that the final figure could exceed **\$17B** as more wallets are identified. {Risk: Medium (structure & compliance), High for specific targeted ecosystems}[^49][^50][^51]
- **Brookings on GENIUS payment stablecoins (2026‑03‑02):** Brookings notes outstanding USD stablecoins rose from about **\$25B** in 2020 to nearly **\$280B** by end‑2025, framing GENIUS‑regulated payment stablecoins as a new systemic dollar funding channel. The paper highlights potential feedback loops between stablecoin reserve demand and U.S. Treasury markets and argues for tighter coordination between prudential and market regulators as supply scales toward the low‑trillions. {Risk: Medium}[^17]

## Security Incidents

- **February hack volume hits 12‑month low:** PeckShield monitoring summarized via KuCoin and MEXC shows **February 2026** saw only about **\$26.5M** in confirmed hack losses across 15 incidents, down **98.2% YoY** from **\$1.5B** in February 2025 and ~69% from January 2026’s ~\$86M. The largest incidents were an ~\$88M exploit on IoTeX’s ioTube bridge, a ~\$10M Stellar‑based YieldBlox exploit (with ~\$7.2M frozen), and mid‑single‑digit‑million losses at CrossCurve, FOOM.Cash, and Moonwell. {Risk: Medium – trend is positive, but bridge risk persists}[^52][^53][^54][^55][^56][^57]
- **IoTeX ioTube bridge exploit (2026‑02‑21):** A single compromised private key controlling IoTeX’s ioTube TokenSafe on Ethereum allowed an attacker between **07:00–09:00 UTC on 2026‑02‑21** to withdraw roughly **\$4.3M** in assets and mint additional wrapped tokens, pushing independent loss estimates above **\$8M**. IoTeX halted the bridge and temporarily paused its L1 chain, coordinated to freeze ~86% of stolen tokens, and pledged full compensation to affected users under a forthcoming governance and mainnet update. {Risk: Medium}[^54][^58]
