### 2. Trust Wallet Browser Extension Breached: \$7M Stolen | **{HIGH RISK}**

Binance-owned Trust Wallet suffered a sophisticated backdoor attack in its Chrome extension v2.68 released December 24, 2025 (12:32 PM UTC). Attackers exploited internal malicious code modification rather than third-party dependency compromise, leveraging a leaked Chrome Web Store API key to bypass Trust Wallet's standard release procedures. Approximately 2,596 wallet addresses were compromised, with attackers stealing ~\$7M in assets and rapidly converting and funneling stolen funds to centralized exchanges—approximately \$3.3M to ChangeNOW, \$340K to FixedFloat, and \$447K to KuCoin—while retaining \$2.8M in cold wallets across Bitcoin, EVM, and Solana chains.[^17]

Trust Wallet patched the vulnerability in v2.69 released December 25, expired all release APIs, and initiated a reimbursement program for verified victims. Approximately 5,000 claims were received, though many were flagged as duplicates or false submissions, requiring wallet-ownership verification for fund recovery. Security researchers suggest the breach may involve nation-state or insider involvement due to the sophistication of API access and internal code modification; Changpeng Zhao, Binance founder, stated the exploit was "most likely" insider-facilitated, though no forensic evidence was presented.[^17]

**Risk Implication**: This incident exposes systemic supply-chain vulnerabilities in wallet infrastructure, particularly reliance on external development environments and API key management. It underscores the importance of evaluating third-party custodians holistically rather than solely on primary security measures.

**Citation**:[^17]

Bitmine Immersion Technologies announced December 28 that its Ethereum holdings reached 4,110,525 ETH (~\$12.1B at \$2,948 per token), representing 3.41% of total ETH supply and positioning the firm as the world's largest corporate Ethereum treasury (second globally only to MicroStrategy's \$59B Bitcoin treasury). As of December 28, Bitmine's total crypto and cash holdings reached \$13.2B, comprising 4.11M ETH, 192 Bitcoin, \$23M in growth-stage equity stakes ("moonshots" including Eightco Holdings), and \$1B in liquid reserves. Currently, 408,627 ETH (~9.9% of holdings) are staked, generating fees at a composite Ethereum staking rate (CESR) of 2.81%, translating to ~\$374M annually or \$1M+ per day at full scale. The company is collaborating with three staking providers to launch its proprietary Made in America Validator Network (MAVAN) in Q1 2026, positioning Bitmine to monetize staking infrastructure at institutional scale.[^23]

**Risk Implications**: Bitmine's concentrated Ethereum positioning introduces liquidity and operational risk. The planned MAVAN infrastructure could face validator consolidation scrutiny from the Ethereum Foundation and regulators, particularly if it captures >5% of network stake.

### 7. Florida Lawmakers Advance Cryptocurrency Reserve Legislation; Fourth U.S. State Initiative | **{MEDIUM RISK}**

Florida State Senator Joe Gruters (R) filed companion bills SB 1038 and SB 1040 on December 30, 2025, establishing the Florida Strategic Cryptocurrency Reserve under the Office of the Chief Financial Officer. Both bills are interdependent and would take effect July 1, 2026, if enacted during the same legislative session.[^27][^28][^29]

**Structure**: SB 1040 creates a dedicated trust fund funded by legislative appropriations, statutory revenue, direct cryptocurrency purchases, blockchain airdrops/forks, and investment earnings from non-crypto holdings. SB 1038 grants the CFO (currently Blaise Ingoglia) broad discretionary authority to acquire, exchange, manage, and retain assets, subject to prudent-investor standards. **Critical Investment Restriction**: The CFO may only purchase cryptocurrencies with average market capitalizations of at least \$500B over the preceding two years—a threshold that currently only Bitcoin satisfies.[^29][^27]

**Oversight**: A five-member Florida Strategic Cryptocurrency Reserve Advisory Committee (chaired by the CFO) oversees management, with biennial reporting requirements to legislative leaders. A built-in sunset clause expires the reserve July 1, 2030, unless renewed by legislature.[^29]

**Why It Matters**: Florida would become the fourth U.S. state—after Texas, New Hampshire, and Arizona (all operational in 2025)—to establish a sovereign cryptocurrency reserve, signaling accelerating institutional adoption and state-level competitive positioning in digital asset markets.

## GOVERNMENT / REGULATION

### U.S. Federal

**SEC Project Crypto** (Chair Paul Atkins): Securities law modernization framework remains in development; "innovation exception" for token offerings expected January 2026 release (delayed from government shutdown).[^31]

**CFTC Digital Assets Pilot** (Acting Chair Caroline D. Pham, December 8): Launched pilot program permitting futures commission merchants (FCMs) to accept Bitcoin, Ethereum, and USDC as customer margin collateral in derivatives trading. Associated guidance clarifies tokenized collateral mechanics and withdrawal of outdated June 2020 "actual delivery" requirements for virtual currency futures.[^32]

**CFTC Letter 25-40** (December 8): No-action relief issued for FCMs accepting stablecoins and non-securities digital assets as customer margin, subject to operational and segregation safeguards.[^32]

**Senate Bipartisan Framework** (Boozman-Booker discussion draft, November 2025): Proposes expanding CFTC exclusive jurisdiction over spot digital commodity markets, requiring broker-dealers, custodians, and trading facilities to register with CFTC and meet core market-integrity obligations (asset segregation, conflicts-of-interest safeguards, cybersecurity standards).[^31]
