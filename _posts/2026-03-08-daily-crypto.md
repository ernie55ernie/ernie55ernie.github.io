## Market Overview (24h)

BTC trades around 68k–67k USD, down ~3% on the day and ~8% over 7 days, with sentiment gauges in “extreme fear” despite still‑strong daily volume (~61B USD). ETH is near 3,700 USD (‑3.4% 24h, ‑9.1% 7d).[^7][^8]
- **Watchlist majors:** Among large caps: BNB 535 USD (‑4.2% 24h), SOL 129.7 USD (‑4.5% 24h, ‑16% 7d), XRP 0.546 USD (‑3.2% 24h), TON 6.80 USD (‑2.1% 24h), ADA 0.522 USD (‑3.9% 24h), AVAX 35.5 USD (‑5.6% 24h), DOGE 0.11 USD (‑6.9% 24h, worst laggard on this list over 7d at ‑22.6%). OP is around 0.12 USD, down about 5–6% over 24h, while ARB trades near 0.47 USD, up ~2.7% on the day but still down ~5.7% over 7 days.[^9][^10][^11][^12][^7]
- **Derivatives:** BTC perpetual funding is modestly positive around 0.0008–0.0009% on major venues, indicating a mild long bias rather than extreme leverage. BTC futures open interest is about 44.7B USD and has fallen roughly 5.4% over the past 24h, consistent with de‑risking after a liquidation wave of ~302M USD in the last day that knocked BTC from above 73k to the 68k area; a more recent 1‑hour snapshot showed ~31M USD in fresh liquidations (predominantly longs).[^13][^14][^15][^16][^2]
- **Flows & ETFs:** U.S. spot Bitcoin ETFs recorded roughly 458M USD of net inflows on 2 March, led by BlackRock’s IBIT (~263M) and Fidelity’s FBTC (~95M), the first positive daily print for March after late‑February outflows. CoinShares’ latest weekly report (week ended around 28 Feb) shows ~1B USD net inflows into digital asset products, ending five straight weeks of outflows (~4B), with BTC (+881M), ETH (+117M) and SOL (+53.8M) dominating.[^17][^18][^6][^1]
- **Stablecoins:** Multiple analyses highlight persistent USDT supply contraction: CryptoQuant estimates the 60‑day change in USDT supply at roughly ‑3B USD, a rare negative reading last seen near the 2022 BTC bottom, and weekly supply reductions around 0.9–1.5B USD in February. At the same time, USDC is taking share in flows: one recent report cites 1.8T USD in March stablecoin volume with USDC accounting for ~70% (1.26T) versus 514B for USDT, and notes that stablecoin balances on exchanges and single‑day inflows (~5.1B on 5 March) have hit multi‑week highs, indicating “reload” liquidity on sidelines.[^19][^20][^21][^22][^23]

## Top Headlines (What & Why it matters)

- **ICE takes strategic stake in OKX at 25B USD valuation** – Intercontinental Exchange (NYSE: ICE) has made a minority investment in OKX, valuing the exchange at ~25B USD and agreeing to license OKX spot crypto pricing while launching U.S.‑regulated crypto futures that OKX will distribute to its 120M‑plus users.[^2][^24][^3]
    - *Why / Risk:* Deepens CEX–TradFi integration and may shift institutional derivatives flow toward regulated venues; short‑term market impact Medium.
- **U.S. spot crypto ETFs see first strong inflows in weeks** – U.S. Bitcoin ETFs attracted about 458M USD net on 2 March (IBIT ~263M, FBTC ~95M, others smaller), while CoinShares tracks ~1B USD net inflows into digital asset funds for the latest week after five weeks of ~4B cumulative outflows.[^18][^6][^1]
    - *Why / Risk:* Signals tentative return of institutional demand after a period of heavy derisking; impact Medium.
- **Hong Kong to issue first fiat‑stablecoin licences next month** – In the 2026–27 Budget, Hong Kong’s Financial Secretary confirmed that the first batch of licences under its fiat‑referenced stablecoin issuer regime will be issued “next month,” alongside plans for the SFC to boost digital‑asset market liquidity, widen products for professional investors, and launch an accelerator.[^5]
    - *Why / Risk:* Positions HK as a regulated stablecoin hub in Asia with near‑term licensing catalysts; impact Medium.
- **SEC settles Justin Sun / Tron case and drops remaining claims** – The SEC filed a proposed final judgment on 5 March in its case against Justin Sun, Tron Foundation, BitTorrent Foundation and Rainberry, dismissing remaining claims as part of a global resolution after a wash‑trading settlement with Rainberry.[^25][^26]
    - *Why / Risk:* Removes a long‑running U.S. enforcement overhang around TRX/BTT but reinforces the Commission’s posture on market‑manipulation; impact Medium.
- **U.S. “landmark” crypto bill hits new impasse in Congress** – Talks on a major crypto‑market structure bill have stalled again after banks refused to back a White House compromise, prompting President Trump to accuse them of trying to sabotage his “Crypto Agenda.”[^27]
    - *Why / Risk:* Increases near‑term regulatory uncertainty around comprehensive U.S. legislation; impact Medium.
- **CME to move crypto futures/options to 24/7 trading from 29 May** – CME Group has confirmed that its regulated crypto futures and options will trade continuously on CME Globex from 29 May 2026 (with only a brief weekly maintenance window), following a 2025 notional volume of ~3T USD and 2026 YTD ADV around 407k contracts (+46% YoY).[^28][^29][^30]
    - *Why / Risk:* Narrows the structural gap between regulated derivatives and 24/7 offshore markets, likely drawing more institutional hedging into CME; near‑term impact Medium, structural impact potentially High.

## Regulation/Law

- **U.S. SEC – Justin Sun / Tron settlement:** As above, the SEC’s 5 March litigation release details a proposed final judgment resolving wash‑trading allegations against Rainberry and dismissing other claims against Sun and related entities in the Tron case.[^26]
    - *Risk:* Medium for TRX/BTT ecosystem; Low for broader market.
- **SEC Crypto Task Force stakeholder input (Coin Center letter):** On 5 March, Coin Center submitted a detailed letter to the SEC’s Crypto Task Force urging the Commission to favor prospective rulemaking over case‑by‑case no‑action/exemptive relief, and proposing a formal safe harbor for decentralized networks plus embedding recordkeeping directly into tokenized systems.[^38][^39]
    - *Risk:* Policy‑shaping Medium (longer‑term); immediate market impact Low.
- **U.S. legislation – stalled compromise bill:** Reuters reports that a “landmark” crypto bill has hit a new impasse as banks rejected a White House compromise, raising doubts about passage in 2026 and drawing criticism from President Trump.[^27]
    - *Risk:* Medium, as it prolongs regulatory uncertainty and could cap U.S. institutional risk‑taking until a clearer path emerges.
- **Hong Kong – stablecoin licensing & broader VA roadmap:** The 2026–27 Budget speech confirms the fiat‑referenced stablecoin issuer licensing regime is in force and that the first licences will be issued next month; the SFC will also enhance market liquidity for professional‑investor products and run an accelerator, while separate commentary notes forthcoming legislation for crypto dealers and custodians.[^40][^41][^5]
    - *Risk:* Medium, positive for Asia‑centric stablecoin and exchange strategies.
- **Japan – FSA cybersecurity standards for exchanges:** Japan’s FSA has published a draft policy introducing mandatory Cybersecurity Self‑Assessments (CSSA) for all registered crypto exchanges, with public comments open until 11 March and implementation planned for the 2026 fiscal year.[^4]
    - *Risk:* Medium for JPY venues (compliance and cost), Low for global prices.

## Security Incidents

- **Solv Protocol double‑mint exploit (~2.7M USD, Bitcoin DeFi):** On 5 March, Solv Protocol disclosed a double‑mint attack against a BRO vault contract that allowed an attacker to inflate an initial 135 BRO into ~567M BRO across 22 transactions, swapping these for 38.05 SolvBTC (~2.7M USD) and draining a BTC‑pegged vault. The team has pledged to fully cover user losses, states that other vaults are secure, and is offering a 10% white‑hat bounty (~270k USD) for fund recovery alongside audits and security upgrades.[^44][^45]
    - *Risk:* High for Solv and BTC‑native DeFi confidence; Low–Medium systemic unless further design flaws or correlated products emerge.
- **Broader context:** February 2026 recorded only ~26.5–35.7M USD in confirmed crypto security losses, the lowest monthly level since March 2025, with the largest individual incidents being a 10M USD oracle manipulation on YieldBlox (Stellar), an IoTeX private‑key compromise (loss estimates 2–9M USD), and a 2.2M USD exploit on privacy protocol Foom.Cash.[^46]
    - *Risk:* Short‑term Low; but the Solv incident shows continued evolution toward sophisticated mint/issuance‑layer exploits.
