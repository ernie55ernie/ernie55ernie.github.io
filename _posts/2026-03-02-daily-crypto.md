## Top Headlines (What & Why it matters)

- [Risk: Medium] **PeckShield: February hacks plunge to 26.5M USD** – PeckShield’s monthly update reports 15 hacking incidents in Feb 2026 totaling ~26.5M USD in losses, a 98.2% year‑on‑year drop versus ~1.5B in Feb 2025 and down 69.2% from ~86M in Jan 2026, suggesting a much quieter exploit environment despite several notable bridge and DeFi incidents.[^4]
- [Risk: Medium] **USDC growth reinforces “regulated stablecoin” narrative** – Circle’s Q4 2025 results show USDC circulation at 75.3B (+72% YoY) with ~733–770M USD in revenue and reserve income, underlining strong institutional and payments‑driven demand for regulated dollar tokens.[^14][^17]
- [Risk: High] **IoTeX bridge private‑key compromise drains an estimated 8–9M USD** – IoTeX’s cross‑chain bridge was exploited on Feb 21 via a compromised private key controlling TokenSafe and MinterPool contracts between 07:00–09:00 UTC, with on‑chain analysts and media estimating roughly 8.8–9M USD in drained and newly minted assets (USDC, USDT, WBTC, PAYG, IOTX, CIOTX, CCS), while the team initially cited ~2M USD in losses and says exchanges are helping freeze funds.[^18][^19][^20][^21]
- [Risk: High] **YieldBlox DAO lending pool on Stellar loses >10M USD in oracle manipulation attack** – On Feb 22, the YieldBlox Blend V2 pool on Stellar suffered an exploit where a single thin‑liquidity USTRY/USDC trade pushed the oracle price ~100× higher (from ~1.06 to ~107 USD), allowing the attacker to borrow roughly the entire pool (~10M USD in USDC and XLM) against overvalued USTRY collateral.[^22][^23][^24][^25]

## Regulation/Law

- [Risk: Medium] **MAS tightens Russia‑related financial measures for DPT providers** – An amendment to MAS Notice SNR‑N01, dated Feb 26 2026 and effective Feb 27, explicitly reiterates that digital payment token service providers are prohibited from facilitating transactions that could help circumvent Singapore’s Russia‑related financial sanctions, reinforcing scrutiny on crypto intermediaries serving sanctioned entities.[^31]
- [Risk: Medium] **Hong Kong SFC obtains worldwide court orders in VA case** – The SFC announced that it has secured worldwide court orders in both Hong Kong and England relating to a virtual‑asset matter, with orders remaining in force until further hearings scheduled on Feb 27 and Mar 6 2026, underscoring the regulator’s willingness to pursue cross‑border relief in VA enforcement.[^32]
- [Risk: Medium] **US Senate inquiry into Binance sanction‑evasion lapses** – Senator Richard Blumenthal, ranking member of the Senate Permanent Subcommittee on Investigations, opened an inquiry following reports that Binance partners Hexa Whale and Blessed Trust allegedly helped launder funds and facilitate trade with Iranian entities, and that Binance compliance had identified about 2,000 Iran‑linked accounts despite the exchange’s public claims of blocking Iranian users.[^33]


## Research/White Papers

- [Risk: Medium] **Messari “The Theses 2026” (recently highlighted Feb 28)** – A widely cited summary of Messari’s annual theses emphasizes seven core segments (Cryptomoney, TradFi, Chains, DeFi, AI, DePIN, consumer apps) and notes that roughly 81% of crypto market cap (~2.63T of ~3.26T USD) is concentrated in assets viewed as “money” (BTC plus monetary‑premium L1s), arguing that most alternative L1s are likely to cede share to BTC as their currency‑premium narratives compress.[^34][^35]


## Security Incidents

- [Risk: High] **IoTeX bridge compromise** – A private key controlling IoTeX’s TokenSafe and MinterPool contracts was compromised on Feb 21 2026 (07:00–09:00 UTC), enabling an attacker to drain multi‑asset reserves and mint additional tokens; estimates of total loss range from ~2M (IoTeX’s initial statement) to ~8.8–9M USD (on‑chain analyst Specter and third‑party coverage), and the team is coordinating with exchanges to trace and freeze funds.[^19][^20][^21][^18]
- [Risk: High] **YieldBlox DAO oracle attack** – YieldBlox’s Blend V2 pool on Stellar was drained of over 10M USD on Feb 22 after an attacker manipulated the low‑liquidity USTRY/USDC market so that the Reflector VWAP oracle reported a ~100× inflated price, allowing excessive borrowing against USTRY collateral and leaving the pool with significant bad debt; post‑mortems attribute the root cause to pool‑side oracle configuration rather than core contract bugs.[^23][^24][^25][^22]
- [Risk: Medium] **CrossCurve bridge exploit (earlier February, still relevant)** – CrossCurve, a cross‑chain liquidity protocol, suffered an estimated 3M USD exploit around Feb 1–2 via spoofed cross‑chain messages that bypassed Axelar gateway validation in a ReceiverAxelar contract, prompting the team to shut down the bridge, identify beneficiary addresses, and offer a 10% white‑hat bounty while threatening legal escalation after 72 hours.[^36][^37][^38][^39][^40]
- [Risk: Medium] **Macro view: exploit losses sharply down in February** – Despite these bridge and DeFi hacks, PeckShield’s monthly data show only 15 incidents totaling ~26.5M USD in Feb 2026, versus ~1.5B USD (dominated by the Bybit hack) in Feb 2025 and ~86M in Jan 2026, indicating a sharp decline in aggregate exploit impact for the month.[^4]

- [Risk: Medium] **Security‑focused X traffic** – Security firms (PeckShield, Halborn, BlockSec, QuillAudits) and IoTeX/YieldBlox teams have used X to confirm and dissect the IoTeX and YieldBlox exploits, with emphasis on key‑management failures and thin‑liquidity oracle design risks, which is pushing dev‑side sentiment toward more conservative oracle and bridge architectures.[^20][^24][^25][^22][^23][^19][^4]
- [Risk: Medium] **TRON ecosystem signal from Tron Inc.** – Tron Inc. (Nasdaq: TRON) announced via press release and follow‑on coverage that it has expanded its TRX treasury to over 681.2M tokens and plans to purchase roughly 50,000 USD of TRX daily for 360 days, positioning itself as the largest public TRX treasury vehicle and reinforcing market perception of sustained institutional‑style demand for TRON block space.[^41][^42][^43][^44][^45][^46][^47][^48]


## What to Watch (next 24–72h)

- [Risk: High] **Arbitrum mega‑unlock on March 16 2026** – A Binance Square post highlights that 1.1B ARB (~76% of current circulating supply) is scheduled to unlock on Mar 16 2026, with ongoing monthly linear releases of ~92.65M ARB on the 16th, implying elevated supply overhang and potential volatility for ARB and the L2 basket as the date approaches.[^49][^50]
- [Risk: Medium] **Follow‑through on IoTeX and YieldBlox exploits** – Monitor announcements on compensation plans, protocol restarts, blacklist actions, and any law‑enforcement or exchange‑driven freezes that could affect secondary‑market liquidity for affected tokens and related ecosystems (IoTeX, Stellar‑based DeFi).[^24][^25][^18][^23][^19]
- [Risk: Medium] **Hong Kong SFC virtual‑asset case hearing on Mar 6 2026** – The continuation of the SFC’s worldwide court orders case around virtual assets, with a hearing scheduled for Mar 6, may yield new precedents on asset freezes, cross‑border cooperation, and investor restitution in VA disputes.[^32]
- [Risk: Medium] **USDC and stablecoin policy narrative** – After Circle’s strong Q4 and USDC supply surge, regulators and policymakers (especially in the US and EU under MiCA) may lean further into stablecoin frameworks; any new guidance or enforcement related to reserve quality, disclosures, or cross‑border use would be immediately relevant for stablecoin‑heavy trading pairs.[^51][^17][^12][^14]
- [Risk: Medium] **Near‑term BTC and ETH ETF flows** – Recent BTC spot ETF flows have turned positive (~0.98B USD net over Feb 24–26) following a prior stretch of outflows; whether this trend persists or reverses this week will be a key driver for BTC directionality and broader risk appetite.[^52][^11]
