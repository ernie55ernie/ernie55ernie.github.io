### TOP HEADLINES

**1. Deribit's Record \$28.5B Options Expiry Sets Stage for Boxing Day Volatility**
{Low-Medium}
Deribit confirmed the largest crypto options expiry in history on December 26, with \$23.6 billion in Bitcoin and \$3.8 billion in Ethereum contracts settling—representing over 50% of the exchange's open interest. Heavy positioning at specific strike levels (especially \$100k–\$116k calls and \$85k puts) will likely pin prices near these levels until expiry, with market makers hedging through spot trades. Holiday liquidity thinning increases the risk of outsized moves once hedging unwinds after settlement.[^8][^1][^7]

**2. Aave Governance Vote on Brand Asset Transfer Opens Mid-Crisis**
{Medium}
Aave Labs unilaterally submitted a Snapshot proposal on December 23 to transfer brand assets (aave.com, social media, GitHub) to the DAO without consulting the proposal's author, Ernesto Boado (former CTO, BGD Labs co-founder), who called the move "shameful" and urged abstention. The vote, scheduled to close December 26, occurs during the Christmas holiday—timing the community disputes as deliberate sabotage. Polymarket odds show only 25% approval probability, yet the protocol's TVL rose \$1.42 billion to \$34+ billion, underscoring the divergence between governance friction and protocol fundamentals. The SEC closed a four-year investigation into Aave on December 2 without enforcement action, potentially clearing regulatory headroom for resolution.[^12][^13]

**3. Uniswap "UNIFication" Governance Proposal Passes with 69M+ UNI Votes**
{Low}
The Uniswap community overwhelmingly approved a landmark governance proposal on December 22–23, securing 69 million UNI votes (vs. 40 million required). The plan includes burning 100 million UNI tokens from the treasury and activating fee switches on Uniswap v2 and selected v3 pools, with a new on-chain mechanism tying protocol burns to usage volumes (deflationary model). Uniswap Labs and the Foundation will consolidate under a unified Wyoming DUNA legal structure. The proposal represents a positive precedent for DeFi governance and value realization.[^14]

**4. UK FCA Publishes Comprehensive Crypto Regulatory Regime (Dec 16)**
{Medium}
The UK Financial Conduct Authority released three consultation papers on December 16, 2025—CP25/40 (cryptoasset activities), CP25/41 (admissions/disclosures/market abuse), and CP25/42 (prudential requirements)—following HM Treasury's finalization of the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (Cryptoassets) Regulations 2025 on December 15. The FCA proposes risk-weighted capital calculations for crypto exposures, with higher weights for volatile/illiquid tokens, and mandatory operational resilience standards. Response deadline: February 12, 2026; full implementation: October 2027. This marks the UK's formal entry into structured crypto regulation, following the EU's MiCA framework.[^15][^16]

**5. Ethereum Fusaka Hard Fork Successfully Activated (Dec 3)**
{Low}
Ethereum completed the Fusaka upgrade on December 3, 2025, marking its most significant Layer 1 update since the Pectra upgrade. The upgrade introduced 12 EIPs focused on scalability, including PeerDAS (allowing nodes to verify smaller data samples, reducing validator load), increased blob capacity for rollups, and strengthened security following a four-week pre-launch audit. Blob capacity was gradually increased over two weeks to ensure validator stability. All infrastructure providers, including NOWNodes, completed upgrades without reported issues. The upgrade substantially reduces Layer 2 transaction costs and improves data efficiency.[^17][^18]

**6. North Korea (DPRK) Continues Crypto Dominance, \$2.02B+ Stolen in 2025**
{High}
North Korean hackers attributed to the Lazarus Group achieved a record-breaking \$2.02 billion in cryptocurrency theft in 2025—a 51% year-over-year increase despite fewer confirmed incidents—according to Chainalysis. The persistence of large single attacks (particularly private key compromises at centralized exchanges) accounts for 88% of theft volumes. A notable exception: Venus Protocol's swift response in September 2025 thwarted a \$13 million attack within 12 hours using Hexagate's proactive monitoring, demonstrating evolving DeFi security infrastructure. Historical context includes the Bybit \$1.5 billion breach in February 2025 (\$300M+ cashed out) and the Coinbase \$400 million hack in May 2025 (Lazarus Group, ~\$1.4 billion in Ethereum involved).[^19][^20][^21]

**7. Bitcoin Spot ETF Flows Mixed; BlackRock IBIT Remains Institutional Anchor**
{Low}
U.S. Bitcoin spot ETF flows turned mixed: the week ended December 13 saw \$286.6 million in net inflows, with BlackRock's IBIT (+\$214.1M) and Fidelity's FBTC (+\$84.5M) leading, while ARK's ARKB (-\$11.1M) and VanEck's HODL (-\$25.2M) saw outflows. However, a three-day period (December 19–21) recorded \$142.09 million in net outflows across the category, with only IBIT positive at +\$6.1 million. Despite a year-to-date loss of 9.6%, IBIT remains the sixth-largest ETF by 2025 inflows at \$25+ billion, highlighting institutional "HODL" behavior during downturns rather than reactive trading.[^22][^23][^24]

### GOVERNMENT / LAW \& REGULATION

**United Kingdom – FCA Crypto Regulatory Framework (Dec 15–16)**
The UK Financial Conduct Authority finalized three consultation papers establishing the regulatory perimeter for cryptoasset firms under FSMA. Key proposals include:[^16][^15]

- Cryptoasset activities (trading, custody, lending, staking) brought under FCA authority.
- Risk-weighted capital requirements with higher weights for volatile/illiquid assets.
- Prudential safeguards (minimum capital, conflicts of interest, disclosure standards).
- Admissions and market abuse regime aligned with traditional securities rules.
Response deadline: February 12, 2026; implementation target: October 2027. **Primary source**: UK FCA consultation papers CP25/40, CP25/41, CP25/42 (December 16, 2025).[^15][^16]

**European Union – MiCA Implementation Status**
The EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) Regulation reached full effect January 1, 2025. As of December 2025, over 90 crypto asset service providers (CASPs) are authorized, and euro-denominated stablecoins are diversifying. Divergent national implementation persists, but ESMA and EBA continue issuing harmonization guidance through regulatory technical standards (RTS) and implementing technical standards (ITS). **Primary source**: Chainalysis 2025 Crypto Regulatory Round-Up.[^29]

**United States – CFTC Spot Crypto Trading \& SEC Staking Safe Harbor**
The CFTC announced on December 4, 2025, that spot cryptocurrency products may begin trading on federally registered futures exchanges (designated contract markets, or DCMs), advancing "Project Crypto." The SEC is expected to release its promised "innovation exception" guidance in January 2026 (delayed from December). Additionally, the IRS issued Rev. Proc. 2025-31 on November 10, providing a safe harbor for trusts to maintain "investment trust" status while staking crypto assets. **Primary source**: CFTC announcement (December 4); JDSupra legal summary.[^30][^31]

**Taiwan – FSC Regulatory Status Quo**
The Taiwan Financial Supervisory Commission has issued VASP registration requirements (mandatory since 2024) and guidelines on asset segregation, KYC, and cybersecurity, but no new material announcements were issued December 16–23. A proposed dedicated VASP law (announced March 2025) remains pending legislative approval. Regulatory clarity is improving, but the crypto-legal framework remains transitional. **Primary source**: Crystal Intelligence, LEX.law.[^32][^33]

**SEC Closes Four-Year Aave Investigation (Dec 2, 2025)**
The U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission terminated its four-year investigation into the Aave Protocol without enforcement action, according to Aave's governance discussion. This regulatory clearance removes a key overhang and may facilitate protocol restructuring discussions.[^12]
