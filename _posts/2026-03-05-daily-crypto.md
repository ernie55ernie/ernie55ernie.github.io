## Top Headlines (What & Why it matters)

- **Kraken secures Federal Reserve master account (rails integration)** – Kraken Financial, its Wyoming-chartered bank, has been granted a Federal Reserve master account by the Kansas City Fed, making it the first digital asset bank/crypto firm with direct access to Fedwire and other core payment rails. This can materially reduce fiat settlement latency and counterparty risk for Kraken clients, and sets a precedent for other crypto institutions seeking similar access. {Risk: High}[^8][^9][^10]
- **CFTC chair signals onshore crypto perpetual futures “within a month or so”** – At a Milken Institute event, CFTC chair Michael Selig said the agency is working to allow “true perpetual futures” for crypto on US venues within roughly the next month, as part of the joint SEC–CFTC “Project Crypto” initiative. This is a major structural shift that could pull leveraged volume back onshore and change competitive dynamics for offshore derivatives platforms. {Risk: High}[^11][^12][^13][^14]
- **US spot Bitcoin ETFs flip back to strong inflows** – Farside data summarized by KuCoin show US spot BTC ETFs saw about 458.2M USD in net inflows on 2026‑03‑02, led by BlackRock’s IBIT (263.2M) and Fidelity’s FBTC (94.8M). The next trading day (2026‑03‑03) added another ~225.2M USD of net inflows, again driven by IBIT even as some peers saw outflows. This supports the narrative that ETF demand is re‑stabilizing after late‑February redemptions. {Risk: High}[^15][^16][^17]
- **Morgan Stanley edges closer to its own spot BTC ETF** – A revised S‑1 filed 2026‑03‑04 (≈18:52 UTC, 10:52 PST) for the proposed Morgan Stanley Bitcoin Trust names Coinbase Custody Trust and BNY Mellon as custodians for fund Bitcoin and cash, respectively. While the fund is not yet approved, this underscores that large US banks are continuing to build ETF pipelines and institutional crypto infrastructure. {Risk: Medium}[^18][^19]
- **Market structure & sentiment from options/derivatives analytics** – Glassnode’s latest Week Onchain (week of 2026‑03‑03) notes that BTC realized profits have compressed by ~63% and futures participation has declined, while ATM implied volatility is slowly normalizing after February’s spike. Positioning remains defensive, with options markets still pricing elevated uncertainty but less panic, consistent with a consolidation regime rather than a new impulsive leg up. {Risk: Medium}[^20]


## Exchanges

- **Binance lists Fabric Protocol (ROBO) and new COPPERUSDT perpetual** – Binance has added Fabric Protocol (ROBO) to various products (Earn, Buy Crypto, Convert, VIP Loan, Margin) and will list a USDⓈ‑margined COPPERUSDT perpetual contract on 2026‑03‑06 (time not yet cited in truncated notice). This expands access to a niche protocol and adds another metals‑linked perp, but impact is localized to those markets. {Risk: Low}[^21]
- **Coinbase to suspend 25 perpetual contracts on 2026‑03‑16** – Coinbase Markets says it will halt trading in 25 perpetual futures (e.g., MET‑PERP, REZ‑PERP, SUSHI‑PERP, GMX‑PERP, GRT‑PERP, ARKM‑PERP) on Coinbase Advanced and Coinbase International around 13:00 UTC on 2026‑03‑16, auto‑settling open positions using the 60‑minute average index price before the halt with final funding set to zero. The move appears aimed at pruning low‑liquidity contracts and concentrating depth, but traders in these perp markets need to manage exposures ahead of the cutoff. {Risk: Medium}[^22][^23][^24][^25]
- **OKX launches USDT‑margined stock perpetuals (NVDA, AAPL, QQQ, SPY, etc.)** – OKX is enabling USDT‑margined perpetual futures on selected US equities and ETFs (NVDA, MU, SNDK, GOOGL, MSFT, AAPL, META, QQQ, SPY) from 2026‑03‑04 07:00–09:00 UTC (15:00–17:00 UTC+8) across its web, app, and API. This deepens cross‑asset products in crypto collateral space and may tighten links between US tech/indices and crypto derivatives flows. {Risk: Medium}[^26][^27]


## Regulation/Law

- **Fed approval for Kraken Financial’s master account** – The Federal Reserve Bank of Kansas City has granted Kraken Financial a limited‑purpose master account, allowing direct access to Fedwire and related services under a phased, one‑year pilot arrangement, without interest on reserves or access to emergency lending facilities. This is a landmark US regulatory decision integrating a crypto-focused institution into core payment infrastructure. {Risk: High}[^9][^10][^8]
- **CFTC moves toward regulated onshore crypto perps & prediction markets** – In public remarks summarized by Bloomberg, CryptoBriefing, and MEXC, CFTC chair Selig said the agency plans to clear a path for onshore “professional” crypto perpetual futures within about four weeks and also advance prediction‑market rules. Combined with the joint SEC–CFTC “Project Crypto,” this signals a pivot from purely enforcement-led policy toward structured rulemaking for derivatives and DeFi. {Risk: High}[^12][^13][^14][^11]
- **OCC’s GENIUS Act stablecoin NPR (just outside 7‑day window but structural)** – On 2026‑02‑25, the OCC issued a 376‑page notice of proposed rulemaking to implement the Guiding and Establishing National Innovation for U.S. Stablecoins (GENIUS) Act, setting licensing, capital, reserve, redemption, and risk‑management standards for “permitted payment stablecoin issuers” under its jurisdiction, including foreign issuers and OCC‑supervised custodians. The rule also operationalizes the Act’s prohibition on paying yield directly or indirectly on payment stablecoins, with a 60‑day comment period roughly through early May; although slightly older than 7 days, it remains central to US stablecoin regulation. {Risk: High}[^28][^29][^30][^31][^32][^33]
- **SEC Crypto Task Force publishes written input submissions** – The SEC has launched a public page posting written input submitted to its Crypto Task Force, increasing transparency into stakeholder feedback as it develops crypto market‑structure and disclosure rules. This is incremental but relevant context for future SEC guidance. {Risk: Low}[^34]


## Research/White Papers

- **Glassnode – “Unsteady Ground, Room to Bounce” (Week Onchain, 2026‑03‑03)** – Glassnode highlights that BTC’s monthly‑averaged realized profits have fallen about 63% as price consolidates below 70k, indicating thinner buy‑side liquidity and “stabilization rather than recovery.” Futures participation has contracted and options IV has compressed after February’s spike, leaving positioning defensive and suggesting that any upside in the near term will likely require fresh spot demand and renewed ETF inflows. {Risk: Medium}[^20]


## Security Incidents

- **IoTeX bridge exploit – compensation phase begins** – IoTeX’s ioTube bridge was exploited on 2026‑02‑21 after a validator owner’s private key on Ethereum was compromised, enabling an upgrade that bypassed security checks, drained roughly 4.4M USD in reserves, and minted 410M CIOTX; mainnet operations were restored on 2026‑02‑24, with 29 attacker addresses blacklisted and about 45M IOTX frozen. The foundation has committed to 100% compensation from treasury funds and opened a claims portal on 2026‑03‑02 where victims deposit affected assets to a Recovery Address and submit on‑chain proofs before receiving reimbursement on Ethereum. {Risk: Medium}[^35][^36][^37][^38][^39]
- **February hacks low but persistent** – A MEXC‑compiled overview reports that total losses from crypto hacks in February 2026 were about 26.5M USD, the lowest monthly figure since March 2025, with incidents including an estimated 3M USD loss at Cross Curve, 2.26M USD at FOOM CASH, and ~1.8M USD at Moonwell. While trend data suggest improving security, the breadth of smaller DeFi incidents indicates continuing smart‑contract and governance‑risk overhang. {Risk: Medium}[^40]
- **Step Finance (Solana) shuts down after prior 27M USD hack** – Solana DeFi aggregator Step Finance announced on 2026‑02‑27 that it is ending operations after failing to recover from a January 2025 treasury wallet breach that stole about 261,854 SOL (~27–29M USD at the time). The closure illustrates long‑tail business continuity risk even when the underlying chain remains unaffected. {Risk: Medium}[^41][^42]

Any indication that Fed access is broadening could be a strong medium‑term narrative for US‑based crypto banking. {Risk: High}[^43][^10][^8][^9]
- **Early CFTC moves on perps and prediction markets** – Given Selig’s “next month or so” timeline, traders should monitor CFTC press room, Federal Register, and major derivatives venues for pilots or no‑action relief enabling onshore crypto perpetuals and new guidance on event contracts. Announcements here could quickly reprice relative valuations for US‑listed derivatives venues versus offshore exchanges. {Risk: High}[^13][^14][^11][^12]
- **IoTeX compensation flows and bridge governance changes** – As IoTeX processes claims and reimburses users in ETH, watch for concentrated selling or repositioning flows, as well as details on governance reforms such as IIP‑55 to decentralize bridge control and introduce multisigs, timelocks, and expanded audits. Similar bridge projects may emulate these controls, affecting cross‑chain infrastructure risk premia. {Risk: Medium}[^36][^37][^38][^35]

Any last‑minute client or infrastructure issues could inject volatility into ETH and major L2 tokens. {Risk: High}[^44][^45][^46][^47]
