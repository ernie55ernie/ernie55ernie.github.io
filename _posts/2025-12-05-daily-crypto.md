- **Yearn yETH exploit aftermath (Risk: Medium)**
    - On November 30, Yearn’s yETH product suffered an exploit involving unlimited minting, draining roughly the entire pool; about 1,000 ETH (≈\$3M at the time) has reportedly been mixed via Tornado Cash.[^15]
    - Security reviews and user‑fund recovery discussions are ongoing; while the absolute loss is modest vs. DeFi TVL, the incident reinforces smart‑contract and composability risk in LST and index‑style products.

    - Balancer was hit in November by a vulnerability in its manageUserBalance logic, allowing an attacker to impersonate users and drain over \$120M across multiple chains.[^16]
    - The size and technical nature of the exploit keep Balancer and similar DeFi primitives under scrutiny and may influence risk premia and insurance costs across DeFi in the near term.
- **Stablecoin reserves and macro footprint (Risk: Low–Medium)**
    - Recent academic and policy work highlights that as of late 2024 Circle and Tether together held over \$100B of U.S. Treasury bills, making stablecoins a material player in short‑term funding markets.[^17]
    - Combined with the post‑October mint wave, this deepens the linkage between crypto liquidity and TradFi money markets, making regulatory or macro shocks to bills funding directly relevant for crypto liquidity.
